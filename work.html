Blew Up A Hospital:

The motive behind the Joker's decision to blow up the hospital is rooted in his anarchic philosophy. Throughout the movie, the Joker seeks to create chaos and prove that anyone, even the seemingly noble and incorruptible, can be pushed into moral compromise or madness. The hospital explosion is a manifestation of his desire to break down societal order and expose the fragility of the moral fabric in Gotham.

The Joker revels in creating situations where individuals are forced to make morally compromising choices or face dire consequences. By targeting Gotham General Hospital, a symbol of order and healing, the Joker aims to show that even places associated with benevolence can be corrupted and destroyed.

The complexity of the plan also reflects the Joker's strategic mind and his ability to manipulate events to achieve maximum chaos, the Joker constantly challenges Batman, Gotham's citizens, and even the criminals, pushing them to question their values and sanity.

Murdering the popular talk show host Murray Franklin:

Arthur, now fully transformed into the Joker, appears as a guest on the Murray Franklin Show, a late-night talk show hosted by Murray Franklin, played by Robert De Niro.During the live broadcast, Arthur, under the guise of the Joker, confronts Murray about the ridicule and mistreatment he has faced throughout his life. As tensions escalate, Arthur becomes increasingly unhinged, culminating in a shocking moment where he shoots Murray on live television, killing him in front of a stunned audience.

This pivotal scene serves as a climax in Arthur's descent into madness and his transformation into the Joker. It highlights the character's nihilistic worldview and his willingness to commit acts of violence as a means of seeking revenge and gaining recognition. The killing of Murray Franklin symbolizes Arthur's rejection of societal norms and his embrace of chaos and anarchy.
